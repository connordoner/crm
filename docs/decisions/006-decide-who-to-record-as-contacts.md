# ADR 6: Who to record as contacts

Date: 26 November 2023

## Status

Accepted

## Context

Following [ADR 5](split-contacts-into-types.md), I now need to decide where to draw the line on which people, organisations and pets I should and shouldn't record.

## Decision

Given that my overall goal is for my personal CRM to be as comprehensive a record as is possible, I intend to record information on every person with whom I have anything more than a fleeting interaction. Whilst it's difficult to say exactly where to draw the line, I think it's reasonable to say that, if I know their name, I ought to record them.

I intend to record two types of organisations: those with whom I have any interactions (such as my doctor, my local supermarket or a business for whom I do ad-hoc work), and to record other organisations so that I can link people to the organisations for whom they work, which will allow me to, for example, find everyone I know at an organisation.

Finally, I intend to record any pets that I know of. In practice, this will likely be pets belonging to my family and close friends. My rationale for this is simple: if I remember to buy a gift for a friend's pet on their birthday, everyone will want to be friends with me!

## Consequences

I will need to consider the attributes and relationships for each type of contact.

Whilst my interpretation is that my processing of personal data is for domestic purposes, and I'm therefore not formally subject to the provisions of GDPR, I'm recording a lot of personal information about those with whom I'm acquainted. It would therefore be prudent to carry out a [Data Protection Impact Assessment](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/accountability-and-governance/guide-to-accountability-and-governance/accountability-and-governance/data-protection-impact-assessments) and consider security and data protection more wildly.
